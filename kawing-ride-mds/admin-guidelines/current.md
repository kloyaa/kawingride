# Admin and Moderator Standards

Last updated: April 10, 2026.

Admins and moderators help Kawing Ride communities stay private, structured, and accountable. Because these roles may involve access to protected onboarding records, trust signals, community settings, and incident reviews, they carry higher expectations than ordinary participation.

These standards apply together with the [Terms of Service](/terms), the [Privacy Policy](/privacy-policy), the [Data Retention Policy](/data-retention), and the [Reporting and Appeals Process](/reporting-and-appeals).

## Role of Admins and Moderators

Admins manage approved communities, access decisions, operating settings, and other governance tasks assigned to them by Kawing Ride or the relevant organization.

Moderators help review incidents, support consistent enforcement, and maintain fair standards across communities. Moderator authority may be narrower than admin authority, but the duty of care is the same wherever protected records or trust-sensitive decisions are involved.

## Use Access Only for Legitimate Platform Purposes

Admin and moderator access exists to support community operations, not personal curiosity or private advantage.

Protected records may be viewed, used, or shared internally only when reasonably necessary for onboarding, verification, dispute review, incident handling, fraud prevention, safety response, or another authorized platform function.

You may not open, copy, export, or re-share protected records because a person is famous, familiar, personally interesting, or in conflict with you.

## Handle Protected Records with Care

Admins and moderators may encounter identity documents, rider verification materials, community onboarding details, access requests, device-risk signals, and report evidence. Those records must be handled confidentially and only within the access granted to your role.

* Do not disclose protected records in public channels or informal chats.
* Do not download or store copies outside approved workflows unless expressly authorized.
* Do not use private information to embarrass, pressure, or retaliate against anyone.
* Report suspected unauthorized access immediately.

## Make Decisions Fairly

Community authority should be exercised with consistency, neutrality, and documented reasoning where appropriate.

Admins and moderators should consider the available facts, the seriousness of the issue, the user's history, and the likely impact on trust and safety. Personal preference, favoritism, rivalry, or pressure from unrelated relationships should not control outcomes.

If you have a conflict of interest in a case, you should step back and ask for another authorized reviewer where possible.

## Respect Product and Pricing Stewardship

Some admins may manage community settings such as pricing guidance, service area rules, onboarding expectations, or access controls. Those settings should be used to support fairness, transparency, and community trust, not to create hidden privileges or arbitrary barriers.

Do not manipulate settings for personal economic advantage or to punish a rider or customer outside a legitimate governance process.

## Incident Review and Moderation Hygiene

When responding to reports or suspicious conduct, admins and moderators should document material decisions in a way that supports later review.

Good moderation practice includes:

* recording what was reported and when;
* noting the evidence considered;
* limiting access to people who need to know;
* applying interim safeguards where a real safety risk exists; and
* avoiding public speculation while a review is ongoing.

## Confidentiality and Security

Admin and moderator credentials must be protected carefully. Shared logins, weak credential handling, and casual disclosure of internal review details are inconsistent with this role.

If you believe your access has been compromised or that another reviewer is misusing sensitive data, report it immediately through the appropriate internal channel.

## Loss of Privileges

Admin or moderator access may be limited, suspended, or permanently revoked if you misuse authority, handle protected data carelessly, create unfair governance outcomes, or otherwise undermine community trust.

Where serious misuse is involved, Kawing Ride may also restrict the underlying user account, preserve records for investigation, or take additional action permitted by the [Terms of Service](/terms).
