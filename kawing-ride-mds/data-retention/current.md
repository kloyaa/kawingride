# Data Retention Policy

Last updated: April 10, 2026.

Kawing Ride keeps records long enough to support safe operations, fair review, and community accountability, but not longer than reasonably necessary for those purposes. This policy explains the retention approach for ride coordination, verification, moderation, and privacy-related records across the platform.

This policy should be read together with the [Privacy Policy](/privacy-policy) and the [Reporting and Appeals Process](/reporting-and-appeals).

## Retention Principles

Kawing Ride's retention approach is guided by a few core principles:

* keep what is needed to operate the service responsibly;
* protect evidence needed for trust, safety, and fraud review;
* remove or de-identify information when detailed personal records are no longer required; and
* preserve records longer when necessary for legal obligations, active disputes, or platform integrity.

## Standard Retention Windows

The timelines below describe Kawing Ride's general operating targets. A longer or shorter period may apply where the facts, the law, or a live safety issue requires it.

### Website Inquiries and Access Requests

General website inquiries, admin access requests, and similar pre-onboarding submissions may be kept for up to 12 months after the request is closed, unless a longer period is needed to investigate misuse, document approvals, or complete a pending onboarding process.

### Customer Account, Profile, and Ride History

Customer profile records and core ride coordination history may be kept while the account remains active and for up to 24 months after account deletion, closure, or the customer's last meaningful platform activity.

This retention window helps Kawing Ride respond to delayed complaints, repeated abuse patterns, account recovery issues, and community-level trust reviews.

### Ride Requests, Negotiations, Confirmations, and Cancellations

Request records, negotiation logs, offer history, confirmation records, cancellation activity, and related timeline data may be kept for up to 24 months after the relevant ride flow closes.

If a request becomes part of a fraud investigation, safety review, or dispute, it may be retained longer until the matter is resolved and any follow-up risk has been assessed.

### Rider Onboarding and Verification Records

Rider onboarding materials, identity checks, vehicle records, and related protected verification files may be kept while rider access remains active and for up to 36 months after the rider relationship ends.

Kawing Ride keeps these records longer than lightweight customer data because rider access carries higher trust and accountability responsibilities, and past verification history may matter in later investigations or repeated misuse reviews.

### Admin and Moderator Authorization Records

Admin and moderator access requests, approval records, protected role-audit trails, and governance activity logs may be kept while the role remains active and for up to 36 months after the role ends.

This helps Kawing Ride preserve accountability for sensitive access decisions and internal review actions.

### Security, Device, and Abuse-Prevention Logs

Security logs, authentication records, device fingerprinting signals, IP-related audit trails, and abuse-detection events may be kept for up to 24 months, and longer where reasonably necessary to investigate coordinated abuse, recurring fraud, or account compromise.

### Reports, Disputes, Appeals, and Enforcement Records

Incident reports, evidence files, internal review notes, appeal submissions, and enforcement records may be kept for up to 36 months after the case is closed.

If the matter presents an ongoing safety risk, repeated misconduct pattern, or legal obligation to preserve records, retention may continue beyond that period for as long as reasonably necessary.

## Deletion, De-Identification, and Aggregation

When detailed personal records are no longer needed, Kawing Ride may delete them, redact them, or convert them into aggregated or de-identified information that no longer reasonably identifies a person.

Aggregated operational insights may be kept for service planning, product improvement, and trust analysis as long as they no longer function as personal data in practice.

## Legal Holds and Exceptions

Kawing Ride may retain relevant records longer than the standard windows when needed to:

* comply with applicable law, lawful requests, or regulatory obligations;
* protect the rights, safety, or property of users, communities, or the platform;
* investigate fraud, abuse, security incidents, or unauthorized access;
* preserve evidence for actual or anticipated disputes, claims, or appeals; or
* complete backup restoration, audit review, or internal control validation.

## How This Works with User Requests

Deletion or access requests are reviewed in light of the retention needs above. Some records may remain even after account closure because they are still needed for security, fraud prevention, moderation history, legal compliance, or the protection of other users.

Kawing Ride will aim to limit retained data to what is still justified, rather than keeping full active-profile access indefinitely.
