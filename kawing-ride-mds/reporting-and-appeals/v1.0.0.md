# Reporting and Appeals Process

Last updated: February 17, 2026.

This archived version records Kawing Ride's initial reporting and reconsideration framework.

## Reporting Issues

Users may report scams, abuse, privacy misuse, unsafe conduct, falsified onboarding, or other activity that appears to violate Kawing Ride rules.

Reports should be made honestly and with enough detail to support review.

## Review Process

Kawing Ride may review reports based on available evidence, severity, urgency, and community impact.

The platform may take temporary protective steps while a review is in progress if doing so appears necessary to reduce harm or preserve evidence.

## Possible Outcomes

After review, Kawing Ride may:

* take no action;
* issue guidance or a warning;
* restrict access;
* suspend a user or role; or
* permanently remove access where the issue is serious enough.

## Appeals

Users who believe an enforcement decision was incorrect may request reconsideration through the available support or review path.

Kawing Ride may uphold, revise, or reverse the earlier decision after reviewing the available information.

## Bad-Faith Reporting

Knowingly false or retaliatory reports are not allowed and may lead to enforcement action of their own.
