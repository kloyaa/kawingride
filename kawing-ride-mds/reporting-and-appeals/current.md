# Reporting and Appeals Process

Last updated: April 10, 2026.

Kawing Ride relies on clear review paths to protect community trust without turning moderation into guesswork. This document explains how reports may be handled, what kinds of interim restrictions may be used, and how a user may request reconsideration of a decision.

This process should be read together with the [Terms of Service](/terms), the [Community Guidelines](/community-guidelines), and the [Data Retention Policy](/data-retention).

## What Can Be Reported

Users may report conduct that appears to violate Kawing Ride rules, including fraud, impersonation, abusive behavior, privacy misuse, unsafe ride conduct, misuse of admin authority, falsified onboarding, platform circumvention, or repeated failure to honor confirmed arrangements.

Reports should be made in good faith and should include relevant context, timing, and supporting evidence where available.

## Initial Review and Triage

Kawing Ride, and where relevant authorized community reviewers, may assess incoming reports based on urgency, credibility, severity, and the risk of ongoing harm.

Cases involving immediate safety concerns, identity abuse, access compromise, or misuse of protected records may be prioritized for faster action.

## Interim Measures

Before a final decision is reached, Kawing Ride may take proportionate interim steps when reasonably necessary to protect users, evidence, or the integrity of the review.

Examples may include:

* temporary account limits;
* role suspension or request removal;
* restricted access to a community or protected workflow;
* preservation of logs and records; or
* temporary communication limits between involved users.

Interim action does not automatically mean a violation has been finally confirmed.

## How Reviews May Be Evaluated

Reviewers may consider:

* the seriousness of the alleged conduct;
* the reliability and completeness of the available evidence;
* pattern of prior behavior;
* whether the conduct appears intentional, reckless, or accidental;
* risk to customer safety, rider trust, community privacy, or protected records; and
* whether a lighter intervention would be enough to prevent future harm.

Kawing Ride may be unable to disclose every piece of internal evidence, especially where doing so would compromise privacy, security controls, or another person's safety.

## Possible Outcomes

After review, Kawing Ride may decide that no action is needed, issue guidance or a warning, remove content, require updated verification, limit access, suspend a role, restrict a community action, permanently remove a user, or take another proportionate measure reasonably tied to the issue.

Some cases may also be referred to community leadership, service providers, or relevant authorities when required or appropriate.

## Appeals

If Kawing Ride takes material enforcement action against your account or role, you may request reconsideration unless the platform states that the decision is final for safety or legal reasons.

Appeals should usually be submitted within 14 days after notice of the decision, unless Kawing Ride states a different deadline in the relevant notice or workflow.

An appeal should explain why the decision may have been wrong, incomplete, disproportionate, or based on new information that was not reasonably available during the initial review.

## Appeal Review Standards

Appeals are not a chance to restart the case from zero without reason. Kawing Ride may focus on whether:

* relevant evidence was missed;
* material facts were incorrect;
* the rule was misapplied;
* the penalty was disproportionate; or
* meaningful new information changes the overall risk assessment.

Kawing Ride may affirm, modify, or reverse the earlier outcome, or may request more information before deciding.

## False, Abusive, or Retaliatory Reporting

Good-faith reporting is protected, but knowingly false, retaliatory, or manipulative reporting is itself a policy violation. Kawing Ride may act against users who weaponize the reporting process to harass others, damage competitors, or gain leverage in negotiations or disputes.

## Recordkeeping and Privacy During Reviews

Kawing Ride may preserve reports, evidence, communications, and review notes for trust, safety, audit, and appeal purposes in line with the [Data Retention Policy](/data-retention).

Information about a case may be shared on a need-to-know basis with authorized reviewers, affected parties, service providers, or authorities where reasonably necessary and lawful.

## Emergencies and External Authorities

If a report involves an immediate threat to life, serious injury, or urgent criminal risk, contact the appropriate emergency or public authorities first. Kawing Ride is not an emergency response service.

Kawing Ride may cooperate with lawful requests from authorities and may disclose relevant information where required by law or where reasonably necessary to protect people from serious harm.
