# Community Guidelines

Last updated: April 10, 2026.

Kawing Ride is meant to make community ride coordination feel calmer, more private, and more respectful. These Community Guidelines set the baseline behavior expected from everyone on the platform, including customers, riders, admins, moderators, and anyone with access to community workflows.

These guidelines complement the [Terms of Service](/terms). Role-specific duties are described in the [Customer Responsibilities and Safety Guidance](/customer-guidelines), [Rider Standards and Responsibilities](/rider-guidelines), and [Admin and Moderator Standards](/admin-guidelines).

## Respect Other People

Treat other users with respect in every part of the ride lifecycle, including onboarding, negotiation, confirmation, support, and dispute handling.

You may not harass, threaten, shame, exploit, dox, intimidate, or discriminate against other people. Hostility that would make someone feel unsafe or unwelcome in a trusted community is not acceptable on Kawing Ride.

## Be Honest and Clear

Trust breaks quickly when people misrepresent who they are or what they are offering. Use real and accurate information, describe requests honestly, and do not manipulate others with misleading claims.

That means no fake identities, no fabricated emergencies, no altered screenshots, no false availability, and no deceptive pricing tactics intended to trap someone after a negotiation begins.

## Protect Privacy

Kawing Ride exists in part because broad public channels often expose too much personal detail. Users must not re-share private ride information, phone numbers, verification documents, screenshots, or incident records outside the intended workflow unless the affected person consents or disclosure is necessary for safety or legal reasons.

If the platform reveals contact details only after a later stage in the ride flow, you must respect that boundary. Do not pressure people to reveal personal information earlier than the product allows.

## Use Negotiation Fairly

Negotiation on Kawing Ride should be structured, respectful, and time-aware.

* Make offers and counteroffers in good faith.
* Avoid spam, repeated pressure, or guilt-based tactics.
* Do not flood a request with duplicate responses or attempt to confuse a customer into accepting the wrong terms.
* Accept that customers may compare responses before deciding.

Rider interest does not entitle a rider to the trip, and customer inquiry does not guarantee a rider will accept. The product works best when both sides let the decision flow do its job.

## Keep the Community Safe

Do not use Kawing Ride for unlawful transport, dangerous conduct, illegal goods, stalking, coercion, or any activity that creates a serious safety risk.

If a situation becomes urgent or dangerous, prioritize emergency or local authority support where appropriate. Kawing Ride is a coordination platform, not an emergency response service.

## No Fraud, Abuse, or Evasion

The following conduct is prohibited:

* Scams, phishing, impersonation, or social engineering.
* Account sharing to evade restrictions or identity checks.
* Circumventing platform controls to avoid moderation, trust review, or accountability.
* Using Kawing Ride to collect leads and then moving activity off-platform in order to hide misconduct that began on the platform.
* Submitting false reports to punish someone, gain leverage, or damage a competitor.

## Respect Community Governance

Admins and moderators help communities operate fairly, but their authority is limited to legitimate platform and community purposes. Community members must cooperate with lawful and reasonable onboarding, verification, and moderation steps.

Admins and moderators must also respect users. They may not use protected access for personal curiosity, retaliation, favoritism, or intimidation.

## Report Problems in Good Faith

If you see fraud, abusive behavior, unsafe conduct, privacy misuse, or repeated bad-faith platform use, report it honestly and with as much relevant detail as you can.

Good-faith reporting helps protect the community. Bad-faith reporting harms trust and may itself lead to enforcement action.

## Enforcement

Violations of these guidelines may lead to warnings, content removal, restricted visibility, temporary suspension, loss of role privileges, permanent removal, or other actions described in the [Reporting and Appeals Process](/reporting-and-appeals).

Kawing Ride will consider safety risk, evidence quality, pattern of conduct, and potential impact on the community when deciding how to respond.
