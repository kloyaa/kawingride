# Customer Responsibilities and Safety Guidance

Last updated: April 10, 2026.

Kawing Ride is designed to reduce the burden customers often carry in informal ride booking. The platform gives customers a more private request flow, clearer negotiation, and stronger accountability signals, but good outcomes still depend on customers using the service responsibly.

These guidelines should be read together with the [Terms of Service](/terms), the [Community Guidelines](/community-guidelines), and the [Reporting and Appeals Process](/reporting-and-appeals).

## Create Honest and Complete Requests

Customers should provide ride details that are accurate enough for riders to respond responsibly. That includes pickup and drop-off context, timing, relevant passenger or baggage details, and any known constraints that may affect the trip.

Do not submit fake requests, placeholder trips, hidden destination changes, or deliberately incomplete details intended to force later renegotiation.

## Use Negotiation Fairly

Kawing Ride is meant to replace chaotic back-and-forth with a clearer decision path. Customers should compare responses fairly, reply within a reasonable time where possible, and avoid using negotiation to waste rider time.

* Do not solicit offers from multiple riders if you already know you will not proceed with the trip.
* Do not pressure riders into unsafe or unrealistic timelines.
* Do not misrepresent competing offers to force an artificial price drop.
* Do not treat a rider's response as a confirmed ride until both sides actually agree.

## Confirm and Cancel Responsibly

If you choose a rider, follow through in good faith. If your plans change, cancel as early as possible and use the available platform flow rather than disappearing without notice.

Repeated no-shows, ghosting after confirmation, or using the platform only to shop for attention may harm your standing in the community and may lead to restrictions.

## Protect Your Privacy

Kawing Ride is designed to keep customer exposure lower than broad public posting, but customers should still avoid sharing more personal data than necessary.

Do not post another person's number, address, identity document, or emergency contact details through community spaces unless you have authority to do so and it is genuinely needed for the ride.

If Kawing Ride offers settings that control when a rider can contact you directly, review those settings carefully and use the option that matches your comfort level.

## Stay Safety-Aware

Platform features and community trust signals can reduce uncertainty, but they do not replace personal judgment.

Customers should:

* Review rider information available through the platform before confirming.
* Use optional safety or contact-notification features when appropriate.
* Avoid pressure to proceed if something feels inconsistent, deceptive, or unsafe.
* Report suspicious conduct instead of trying to privately "test" a suspected bad actor.

If a situation becomes urgent, contact the appropriate emergency or local authorities. Kawing Ride is not an emergency dispatch service.

## Payment and Trip Conduct

Unless Kawing Ride launches a separate in-app payment feature, customers are responsible for understanding the agreed payment terms with the rider.

Do not refuse payment after a completed ride without a legitimate reason, and do not use threats, public shaming, or fabricated complaints to avoid a fair obligation. If a dispute exists, document it honestly and use the platform's reporting path.

## Report Issues Honestly

If a rider behaves deceptively, violates privacy, creates a safety concern, or fails to honor agreed terms, report the matter with accurate details and relevant evidence where available.

False reports can damage trust and may lead to enforcement action against the customer who submitted them.
